These typically furry and cuddly animals were considered sacred in ancient Egypt. Today, with over<span class="amp-wp-inline-329fdb7771c10d07df9eb73273c95a60"> 74 million cats living in over 36 million American households</span><span class="amp-wp-inline-329fdb7771c10d07df9eb73273c95a60">, many have welcomed our feline overlords — in real life and on the internet.</span></p><p><span class="amp-wp-inline-329fdb7771c10d07df9eb73273c95a60">Only the continental U.S. was considered, and stray or feral cats</span><span class="amp-wp-inline-329fdb7771c10d07df9eb73273c95a60">, which are estimated to number well over 50 million, were not counted. Based on the latest data from the American Veterinary Medical Association, an estimated 30.4% of households own cats — or is it the cats that own the households? </span></p><p><span class="amp-wp-inline-329fdb7771c10d07df9eb73273c95a60">The region with the highest cat-owning households per capita is New England — the area consisting of Connecticut, Maine, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, Rhode Island, and Vermont. With 35.0% of households, compare this to the 28.5% of households that own cats in the South Atlantic, the region with the lowest percentage of cat-owning households. Still, the states of the South Atlantic — Delaware, Florida, Georgia, Maryland, North Carolina, South Carolina, Virginia, and West Virginia — own the largest sheer number of cats as pets.</span></p><p><span class="amp-wp-inline-329fdb7771c10d07df9eb73273c95a60">24/7 Wall St. ranked the states based on household cat ownership rates.
